INCLUSION OF TRANS PEOPLE IN THE
WORK ENVIRONMENT

There was a significant change in this area.
Of the companies that were being monitored by HRC
(Human Rights Campaign), in 2002, zero companies had completely
inclusive health insurance for trans people, and
nowadays we have 79%.
And there are other ways for a company to support the
people that are going through a transition at work,
like facilitating the name change in the company documents
or allowing them to express their gender according to the dress code
that makes sense to them. In general, education and
training are fundamental so new measures can work,
as well as the dialogue and conversations that open
space so people can understand trans colleagues and even trans clients.

HOW TO CHANGE YOUR LEGAL NAME

To do the change is necessary to present your birth
certificate, a copy of your ID, a copy of your social
security number, voter registration, proof of
residence, and a marriage certificate.
To duplicate your birth certificate can be solicited
on the civil registry, preferably in the unit the person
was registered, where the price of the document
is lowered, once it is not necessary to cover the
transporting taxes between offices.

TRANS PEOPLE DISCRIMINATION

Although there are laws protecting the transgender group,
the rights of this community are still not respected.
According to a survey by Antra
(National Association of Transvestites and Transsexuals),
Brazil is the country that kills the most transsexuals in the world for the
13th consecutive year. The transphobia of the pathologization
of gender identity and exclusionary actions against
the group prevent the exercise of full citizenship by
trans people and the free expression of their individualities,
generating violent and systematic discrimination.
Laws have the effect of de-neutralizing violence,
in addition to reversing the scenario of injustice and impunity.

ABOUT THE TRANSBORDAMOS ASSOCIATION

Transbordamos is a non-profit association dedicated exclusively to the empowerment of Transgender (Trans) people, which has the fight against violence as part of its DNA and works to promote and guarantee the rights of these people, generating inclusion in all spheres of society, in health, education, culture, social, legal, entrepreneurship and financial autonomy. Founded in 2019 in the city of São José dos Campos, São Paulo, Brazil, Transbordamos has already impacted more than 2,000 Transgender people in the Paraíba Valley, winner of the first Juntas Transformamos award from the Avon Institute and nominated for the Festival Vale da Diversidade 2022 award. Recognition by Google, Brazilian women's group and was featured in the main magazines and newspapers in Brazil and abroad. Learn more about the project through the social networks @transbordamosassociacao and come "transbordar" with us.
