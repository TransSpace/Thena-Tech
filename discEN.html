TRANS PEOPLE DISCRIMINATION


The movie “The Danish Girl” tells a true story about the transformation process of the artist Elinar Wegener, who discovered himself as trans in the 1920s and was renamed Lili Elbe. She underwent one of the world's first sex reassignment surgeries. Since the beginning of her transformation, she has suffered various types of prejudice, including physical aggression, for the simple fact of leaving her house in cross-dressing. After almost a hundred years, the world still faces discrimination against transsexuals, transvestites and transgender people. This behavior is due to a patriarchal society. Given this scenario, it is necessary to guarantee public safety for trans people in Brazilian territory.

The patriarchal and sexist society is one of the causes of this problem. According to Heleieth Saffioti, a Brazilian sociologist, the patriarchal system is exercised by the heterosexual cisgender white man. This happens through the creation of people within the regime of heteronormativity with the construction of well-defined gender roles. This retrograde mindset is passed on from generation to generation. This way, the strengthening of this ideology of exclusion, in addition to compromising human and social development, also exacerbates the problem in Brazil.

Although there are laws protecting the transgender group, the rights of this community are still not respected. According to a survey by Antra (National Association of Transvestites and Transsexuals), Brazil is the country that kills the most transsexuals in the world for the 13th consecutive year. The transphobia of the pathologization of gender identity and exclusionary actions against the group prevent the exercise of full citizenship by trans people and the free expression of their individualities, generating violent and systematic discrimination. Laws have the effect of de-neutralizing violence, in addition to reversing the scenario of injustice and impunity.

It is inferred, therefore, that transphobia took root in Brazil by a conservative society that neglects acts of violence against trans people. As a form of combat, the Federal Government together with the Public Power must carry out and promote campaigns of national scope as a way of encouraging respect for diversities and the implementation of specialized police stations in crimes of a homophobic nature in favor of the safety of this community and the fight against transphobic violence.
