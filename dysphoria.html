GENDER DYSPHORIA


Gender dysphoria refers to psychological distress from an incongruence between one´s sex assigned at birth and one´s gender identity. People with gender dysphoria usually feel trapped in a body that doesn’t look like their own and may experience a lot of stress, anxiety and depression. Psychotherapy, hormone therapy and gender affirmation surgery may help with the distress.

The diagnosis is made by a psychologist, but evaluation by an endocrinologist, nurses and social worker is recommended so that the evaluation takes place in an empathic and welcoming way. Dysphoria is usually confirmed when a person feels for 6 or more months that their sex is incompatible with their gender identify. It is critical that the person receives support from friends and family during the process.

Psychotherapy helps people to deal with their suffering and understand their feelings, as they are more vulnerable to mental disorders, due to the social context such as stigma and discrimination.

Hormone therapy decreases the production of sex hormones, minimizing secondary sex characteristics at birth and inducing desired sex characteristics. It is necessary to have an evaluation of an endocrinologist and psychologist, due to its side effects, which can affect the person's health and self-esteem.

Surgical treatment is a time-consuming process as it is an irreversible procedure. Gender affirmation surgery consists of remodeling and removing some organs and to be performed, the person must have a follow-up of at least two years with a psychologist and an endocrinologist, to confirm that the new physical identity is indeed suitable for the person.
