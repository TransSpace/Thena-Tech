HOW TO CHANGE YOUR LEGAL NAME

To do the change is necessary to present your birth certificate, a copy of your ID, a copy of your social security number, voter registration, proof of residence, and a marriage certificate. To duplicate your birth certificate can be solicited on the civil registry, preferably in the unit the person was registered, where the price of the document is lowered, once it is not necessary to cover the transporting taxes between offices.

TRANS PEOPLE DISCRIMINATION

Although there are laws protecting the transgender group, the rights of this community are still not respected. According to a survey by Antra (National Association of Transvestites and Transsexuals), Brazil is the country that kills the most transsexuals in the world for the 13th consecutive year. The transphobia of the pathologization of gender identity and exclusionary actions against the group prevent the exercise of full citizenship by trans people and the free expression of their individualities, generating violent and systematic discrimination. Laws have the effect of de-neutralizing violence, in addition to reversing the scenario of injustice and impunity.

GENDER DYSPHORIA

Gender dysphoria refers to psychological distress from an incongruence between one´s sex assigned at birth and one´s gender identity. People with gender dysphoria usually feel trapped in a body that doesn’t look like their own and may experience a lot of stress, anxiety and depression. Psychotherapy, hormone therapy and gender affirmation surgery may help with the distress.

SOCIAL NAME

The social name is the name of the transgenders and how they identify themselves. It is how they choose to be called, accordingly to their gender identity. It represents who the citizen is and it reflects your gender identity. Social name recognition is a right conquered by trans people, who daily face the embarrassment of being called by a name that represents a gender with which they don’t identify.
